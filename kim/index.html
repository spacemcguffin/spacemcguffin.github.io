Building this new site - and, particularly, working on the timeline page - got me thinking about my first ever website. I built it when I was ten years old using Microsoft's WYSIWYG editor Front Page 2000, hosted on Tripod (an alternative to Geocities at the time).

It looked like this:

There is something very charming about those early personal websites: earnest, cluttered, handmade, and entirely unconcerned with best practice. They felt more like bedrooms than brands.

Why I still love this style

Even though this updated design is much cleaner, it still carries some of that same personal warmth. The oversized serif typography, the pale paper background, and the generous spacing all make it feel reflective rather than corporate.

Good personal websites feel like a person is speaking, not a content system.

That is probably why this kind of layout works so well for essays, timeline posts, diary entries, and nostalgic writing.
