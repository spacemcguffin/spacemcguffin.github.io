Building a quieter version of lewis.lc

April 5, 2026 • Design / personal site

I wanted to see what would happen if I stripped everything back a little. Less glow, less layering, fewer effects — but still something that felt personal. Not clinical, not like a template, and not especially “content-first” in the usual sense.

A lot of my pages lean into atmosphere: neon gradients, glass surfaces, blur, depth, movement. I still love all of that. But I also like the idea of a page that feels closer to paper — something slower, softer, and a bit more reflective.

This layout sits somewhere between a blog post, a diary entry, and an archive page. It keeps the wide breathing room and restrained navigation from the original inspiration, but shifts the branding and tone toward lewis.lc.

Why this works

The nicest thing about this kind of design is that it gives the words room to breathe. The page feels authored rather than assembled. There is enough framing to feel intentional, but not so much that the layout starts competing with the writing itself.

Not every personal page needs to announce itself. Some of them can just invite you in.

I could imagine using this for diary posts, archive writeups, retrospective pieces, old internet memories, or longer articles that do not need the full neon treatment.

— Lewis
