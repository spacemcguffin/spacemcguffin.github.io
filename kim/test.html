A quieter editorial page for lewis.lc

April 5, 2026 • Inter + Onest • Light / dark mode

A lot of my site leans into atmosphere: glow, blur, glass, depth, colour, movement. I still like all of that. But I also wanted a version of lewis.lc that felt calmer and more editorial — something with a little breathing room, softer pacing, and typography doing most of the work.

This version keeps the minimalist structure of the reference, but shifts the voice closer to my own site. The serif has gone. Instead, the page uses Onest for titles and Inter for body copy, which makes everything feel a bit cleaner, more modern, and more in line with the rest of my pages.

I also wanted a hint of the usual Lewis.lc colour language, but without dragging the page fully back into neon territory. So the background carries a very soft purple/cyan wash, just enough to tie it into the wider site.

What changed

The main shift is in tone. The page is still spacious and restrained, but it now feels closer to the rest of your design language. The navigation is subtle, the title has more of that Onest personality, and the colour sits between warm paper and faint neon.

The goal was not to remove personality — just to let it speak more quietly.

Light and dark mode both work from the same idea. In light mode, the page feels soft and slightly pearlescent. In dark mode, it becomes moodier and more atmospheric, with the same purple/cyan tint lifting the background.

This kind of template would work really nicely for diary entries, personal essays, archived internet writeups, retrospectives, or any longer post where you want the page to feel authored rather than assembled.

— Lewis
