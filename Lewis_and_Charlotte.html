Lewis & Charlotte
Title Lewis & Charlotte
No. of series 3

Format Webseries, blog posts

Lewis & Charlotte (sometimes stylised as Lewis and Charlotte) was a series of online short stories told via webcam screenshots, captions and image macros.

The stories predominantly featured best friends Lewis and Charlotte doing random things, having bizarre adventures and meeting guest characters. As the stories were impromptu and unscripted, many of the narratives were often surreal and occasionally incohesive or random.

Creation

IRL friends Lewis and Charlotte would often spend evenings after school talking on MSN Messenger and, by extension, go live on webcam to each other. Whilst goofing around, the pair began taking screenshots of each other. When Lewis received a bunch of them, he decided to make memes using them inspired by lolcats and I Can Haz Cheezburger?.

It then dawned on him that he could use these captions to invent a narrative, and the first Lewis & Charlotte story was born. At the time, this was a one-off, and sent to Charlotte privately. Shortly after, Lewis made another story from other screenshots, and Charlotte then made her own too using crappy MSPaint. In order to archive them, Lewis registered a Blogspot account to host the stories.

Development

Over time, the friends created more and more stories, sometimes with ongoing narrative strands, running gags, and callbacks.

Once 9 of these stories had been made, Lewis then rounded the series off with a 10th episode 'series finale'. Of the first 10 episodes, Lewis is known to have created and edited 8 episodes, with Charlotte creating 2. The series was then shared with a couple of close friends at school, and soon the pair decided to continue with the project and began a second series.

The production of the episodes remained largely the same. Lewis and Charlotte would goof around on webcam, take a bunch of screenshots at random, and then arrange them into stories. Occasionally, one of the friends would suggest a story idea and thus the webcam photos were a little more structured and pre-planned.
