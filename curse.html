The Curse of Fenric

Parts One and Two

The Loch Ness monster seems to makes an appearance at the start of this story, quite why I am not sure. No hang on its not Nessie its just one of those Viking longboat thingies shot rather interestingly from under water. The opening scenes of this story are rather atmospheric complete with underwater filming. It is hard to credit that this was directed by the same man who bought us the over lit mess that is called Paradise Towers. There is some fine direction on display I this story, it really looks quite cinematic (considering the budget) and it is not surprising at all that this story works better when watched in the extended feature length version. There are a nice use of filters during the scene where the Russian soldier is attacked by something unknown! They certainly got their money's worth from the underwater camera in this episode. I don't think they used it this much until The Impossible Planet.

It is interesting that the Russian soldiers are speaking in their native tongue in the opening scenes. Was this just to prove that they had got actual Russian actors to play them rather than just any old British actor. But then they start speaking in English. Why bother? I wonder if they could only afford the translator for a very short time, which is why the Russian soldiers are speaking in English for the majority of the story. It would have been interesting if they had spoken in Russian throughout.

Mark Ayres music is always interesting and really adds to the atmosphere of the episode even though at times it can be a little overbearing.

Then all of a sudden Nicholas Parsons appears playing a vicar. He is actually rather quite good all things said and it is easy to forget that he did in fact used to be an actor and was not the just the bloke who used to present Sale of the Century. I remember thinking at the time that it was a bit strange having Nicholas Parson in Doctor Who but he was actually one of the best things in it.

Sophie Aldred is looking very fetching in her 1940s get up, well apart from that hair net anyway. How no one notices that Ace is wearing her own coat over her 1940s get up in some scenes with all its modern day paraphenalia is anyones guess. Perhaps they just aren't that observant or perhaps the script writer just thought no one would even notice.

I wonder if the preponderance with chess is going to be important later or perhaps Commander Millington is trying to win the base's chess tournament by not allowing anyone else to practice. Or perhaps he is more of a backgammon man.

Onto part two and the resolution to a rather insipid cliff-hanger (so it wasn't just the new series that couldn't do decent cliff-hangers after all). It's quite good that they forgot to pay the electric bill when this and Ghost Light were made as the low lighting definitely does add to the atmosphere of the piece.

There's a line in this episode that I think sums up Doctor Who fandom at that time "No girls".

Swimming seems to be used a metpahor for sex in this story what with the two evacuee girls saying to Miss Hardaker "Just because you've never been swimming. Kind of like dancing in the new series. When the two girls go back into the water later they come back all goth with long fingernails and stuff asking this soldier to play with them! Saucy!

Actually, if you think that is a bit near the knuckle then you should read the novelisation. That is full of sexual subtext. It isn't really alluded to much in the broadcast story but in the novel it certainly is so I think that was always in Ian Briggs mind.

The cliffhanger is much better this time with the Sea Devils moment when the Haemovores walk out of the water. That's how you do it.
