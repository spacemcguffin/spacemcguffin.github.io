Lewis & Charlotte (sometimes stylised as Lewis and Charlotte) was a series of online short stories told via webcam screenshots, captions and image macros.

The stories predominantly featured best friends Lewis and Charlotte doing random things, having bizarre adventures and meeting guest characters. As the stories were impromptu and unscripted, many of the narratives were often surreal and occasionally incohesive or random.

Creation

IRL friends Lewis and Charlotte would often spend their evenings after school talking on MSN Messenger and, by extension, appear live on webcam to each other. Whilst goofing around, the pair began taking spontaneous screenshots. When Lewis received a bunch of them, he decided to make memes using them inspired by lolcats and I Can Haz Cheezburger?.

It then dawned on him that he could use these captions to invent a fictional narrative, and the first Lewis & Charlotte story was born. At the time, this was a one-off, sent to Charlotte privately. Shortly after, Lewis created another story from other screenshots, and Charlotte then joined in, making her own using MS Paint.

In order to publish and archive the project, Lewis registered a Photobucket account to host the images, and set up a Blogspot site to display the stories.

Development

Over time, the friends created more and more stories, sometimes with ongoing narrative strands, running gags, and callbacks. Once 9 of these stories had been made, Lewis then rounded the series off with a 10th episode 'series finale' (believed to be called The End, and later retitled The End of the Series).

Of the first 10 episodes, Lewis is believed to have created and edited 8 episodes, with Charlotte creating 2 mid-series. The blog was then shared with a couple of close friends at school, and soon the pair decided to continue with the project and begun a second series.

The production of the episodes remained largely the same. Lewis and Charlotte would goof around on webcam, take a bunch of screenshots at random, and then arrange them into stories. Occasionally, one of the friends would suggest a story idea and, as a result, some of the webcam photos would be a little more structured and pre-planned. Episodes would be released spontaneously, with no fixed schedule.

Visual style

Lewis' episodes were often created using online image macro generators. Adhering to the style of memes of the time, large white Impact font was used for dialogue and captions. Charlotte's less advanced method of creating captions involved adding dark, small fonts on MS Paint. Due to their difference in approach, it was obvious which of the friends had produced each episode.

Guest appearances

Whilst the series focused mostly on Lewis and Charlotte, occasionally a guest would appear in a cameo role. Charlotte's friend Leah appeared in the second episode of the second series, Goode Goes to Greenland, and reappeared later in the same series. It is also believed that Charlotte's other friend, Kirsten, made a cameo too, but this is disputed and unconfirmed.

None of Lewis' friends made appearances. In his frames, only he appeared. Other guests only showed up alongside Charlotte.

Format

There were 3 full series of Lewis & Charlotte in total, followed by a handful of specials which were tentatively referred to as Series 4. Each series was made up of 10 individual episodes, and it is believed that just a handful of specials were made following the original end of the project.

Episodes

All of the episodes are now lost media. All traces of the original project have since been lost or deleted. Below is a list of known / believed episode titles, although the list is far from complete.

  • End of An Era [disputed]

    • Book version

    As a high school leaving gift to Charlotte, Lewis printed each screengrab and created three A6 sized flip-books as physical keepsakes. These were the only printed copies, and the series was never published in any official capacity. The current status of these books is unknown, and they are believed to be lost or since destroyed.
