losing the plot

28 November

It’s snowing, it’s freezing, and it’s the penultimate episode of Doctor Who Series 13 today. I wrote this little ramble earlier:

the flux, the time force, the division, the lupari, sontarans, the grand serpent, passenger, swarm, azure, planet time, time vs space, bel, vinder, Kate, UNIT, non-entities Dan and Yaz, jericho, fugitive doc, b&w house... this series of Doctor Who has just utterly lost me. what are the stakes? is the flux still a threat? what is the b&w house? what is swarm’s goal or motivation? wtf is the serpent? why does nobody care the universe is destroyed? what is the time force? also there’s a random victorian man, and mouri, the doctor is missing memories. There’s just far too much STUFF smashed together with no focus or reason to care about much, if any of it. and now we have UNIT back too, as if there wasn’t enough going on to satisfactorily wrap up (lol)

I’m just frustrated that we’ve been here before with endless set ups and naff resolutions. I have zero hope any of this will properly gel together. There’s no reason to invest in any of the plot – there’s too much plot, and most of it’s being expositioned to us as usual. ah well

I can’t be bothered structuring that into a more cohesive review or ramble. If the production team can’t be arsed making a good show, then I can’t be arsed pouring any more effort into it than that.
