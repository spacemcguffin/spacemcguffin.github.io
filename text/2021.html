10 February

2:30am. The night sky is so clear tonight. An abundance of stars just littering the sky. So pretty. We all get so wrapped up in politics and work and technology and anxiety and other people that we forget just how beautiful this planet and this universe is.

Poked my head out of the window. Felt the winter breeze on my chest. A faint sound of a dog barking and an owl hooting. But the stars, gently flickering in the sky. The world asleep, so quiet, so calm.

19 January

16 January

Covid has set me right back, just as I’d gained a new sense of optimism and drive. Lockdown has re-isolated me, pushed me back into a shell, trapped me in four walls for days and weeks at a time. Constantly wavering between having an identity crisis, and just distracting myself completely to avoid the world and any sense of responsibility.

4 January

It’s officially almost-proper lockdown, again. Lockdown #3. New year, same shit. Everyone else in the house is still at work. I’m on furlough again. It’s a never-ending nightmare. The loneliness sucks. I just want hugs, and a drink at Spoons, please.
