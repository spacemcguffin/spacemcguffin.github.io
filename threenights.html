winter breeze

february 10th, 2021
2:30am

The night sky is so clear tonight. So many stars just littering the sky. So pretty.

We all get so wrapped up in politics and work and technology and anxiety and other people that we forget just how beautiful this planet and this universe is.

Poked my head out of the window. Felt the winter breeze on my chest. Faint sounds of a dog barking, and an owl. Stars gently flickering in the sky. The world asleep, so quiet, so calm.



snow-dusted rooftops

february 11th, 2021
2:30am

Fewer stars in the sky tonight, but those visible are as bright as ever. The same winter chill on my chest. Snow dusting the tops of houses behind us. The frost a sharp contrast against the heat of my radiator.

Absolute silence tonight. You could hear a pin drop. Absolute silence and peace. Momentary bliss. Being a night owl isn’t always a bad thing.

At the start of this pandemic, at the start of lockdown, I’d spend many nights at my window wondering when it might end. Wondering how long I’d be stuck at home for. Wondering when the world would go back to normal. I remember how naive we all were, nearly a year ago. The world is different now. But I’ve learnt, and experienced, and come to somewhat accept it.

There’s something quite fairytale about snow-dusted rooftops against a starry sky. It’s nice to just let go and feel like a kid again. A world full of wonder and magic. I should enjoy the night sky more often. It does wonders for the soul.



chimes of midnight

february 12th, 2021
2:00am

No stars tonight. A sky covered in clouds. But still bliss, and the winter air blesses my skin.

Opened my window just in time to hear the church bell too. This house is the first one I’ve lived in that’s been close enough to hear a church bell in the middle of town, and I’m gunna miss it when I move. Everyone else is asleep but the bell still chimes. Maybe there are others at their window, enjoying the same experience. That’d be nice.
