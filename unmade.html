Lost in the Light Dimension

Lost in the Light Dimension
Featuring The Light Guardian
Written By Gavin Spacey

The Doctor ends up in an alternate reality where everything is very hard to see because it’s too bright, and he manages to lose the TARDIS.

With the universe getting ever brighter, will the Doctor be able to find the light switch at the end of the universe and fix the brightness?

Notes:

    • The Space Lords

    The Space Lords
    Featuring Time Lords, Space Lords
    Written By Phoenix Rising

    The Doctor discovers that there are seemingly random spacial anomalies scattered all throughout the universe. Everything keeps being moved to the wrong place, planets are either too close or far away from suns. In short, the universe is a total mess and it's very inconvenient to move around in.

    After wrongly assuming that this ludicrous situation was caused by the Time Lords, they inform the Doctor that all of this chaos was caused by a new enemy - an alien race known as The Space Lords. This naturally irritates pretty much everyone since it just sounds like they're ripping off the Time Lords, and moving everything around is just annoying in general.

    Notes:

  • This story was cancelled.

    • Quest of the Daleks

    Quest of the Daleks
    Featuring Daleks, Davros
    Written By Dussell T Ravies

    After the events of Remembrance of the Daleks, the Daleks want revenge on the Time Lords and have a pretty heated discussion over what to do. Davros decides to destroy Gallifrey and steal their unlimited rice pudding.

    The Doctor finds out the plan so he and his pet tortoise, Bob, decide to stop the Daleks and keep the unlimited rice pudding away from Davros' old wrinkly hands.

    • Fridge of the Cybermen

    Fridge of the Cybermen
    Featuring Cybermen
    Written By Probably David Banks

    The TARDIS crash lands in a house. After knocking out the home owner, the Doctor decides to raid the fridge, looking for some pickled onions, something which the TARDIS desperately requires to function. But upon opening the fridge, rather than food, he finds a Cyberman.

    As it turns out, the Cybermen have come up with a dastardly scheme to invade Earth by hiding in a new brand of fridge, which are actually disguised singular Cyber-tombs. Closing the fridge, the Doctor takes a phone from the home owner and orders a product recall for the fridge, ending the threat.

    Notes:

  • This story was cancelled.

    • The Crown of Flames

    The Crown of Flames
    Featuring King Flopsworth
    Written By Iydat Prhapbhy

    The Doctor lands in medieval England where an undocumented king rules Britain. He tries to investigate, but instead is captured. He creates an elaborate plan to overthrow the king, whom he has determined to have hypnotised the population using his crown.

    The Doctor executes his plan, ending up with the crown, revealing the king as a holographic simulation based on the thought processes of a hyper-intelligent rabbit. To stop the rabbit, the Doctor's only choice is to wear the crown. After doing so, his head sets on fire. Using this fire, he manages to burn the rabbit to death.

    Notes:

  • This story was cancelled.

    • In the Forest of the Drashigs

    In the Forest of the Drashigs
    Featuring Drashigs
    Written By Oliver Burgin-Plews

    The TARDIS materialises in a forest on Earth in 2018 to find it over-run with lots of Drashigs, who nearly eat him. Whilst working out how to get rid of them, he discovers that a meteorite is about to strike the Earth. Unable to find a way to stop it, he braces for the end, only for the Drashigs to ride up into the air and head it away, before celebrating and stating they have won 'the tournament'.

    The Doctor realises he landed in time for Drashig United's crucial penalty save and accidentally invaded the pitch.

    Notes:

  • This story was cancelled.

    • The Threads of Destruction

    The Threads of Destruction
    Featuring The Master
    Written By Ben Spector

    The Master tries to eliminate the Doctor by replacing his usual coat with a passively evil “Dupli-coat”. This coat transmats anything put into its pockets to the Master, subtly disarming the Doctor, and emits a low-level psychic field that will influence its wearer to pilot the Doctor’s TARDIS into a deadly trap.

    After a wardrobe mix-up with his companion, the Doctor realises the true nature of the “Dupli-coat”. Managing to evade the trap, the Doctor sends a dangerous alien artifact through the coat's pocket that will 'deal with' the Master.

    Notes:

  • This story was cancelled.
