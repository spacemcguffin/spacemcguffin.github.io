timelordwannabe

Fifteen long years ago today, 17 December 2009, I was messing about with my old school bag, putting Christmas presents into the boot of my car, when I spotted some writing on the inside of my bag. It said, ‘You may never realise this but meh, your my best friend + i loves you lotsh :)’ – and it was written by Charlie. Bloody hell. Fifteen years. Half my lifetime ago.

I only know that cos I have a tiny handful of diary entries from December 2009. Reading them back is slightly cringeworthy. Another point of note is that I came up with my old username, timelordwannabe, on 21 December 2008. Sixteen years ago. Prior to that, between February and December, I believe my 2008 diary was called ‘from the mind and keyboard of lewis’ but god knows what my shorter username was.

    Making music

    Currently listening to Farewell 42, an AI-generated song of mine. I saw someone post an AI-generated song on Twitter the other day and I had to give it a go. Ended up buying a load of credits, so I’ve had a blast generating a few different songs.

    You can input a prompt and it’ll make an entirely new song from scratch, complete with vocals, in a genre of your choice. You can also input your own lyrics. I decided to generate a punk rock song about Charlotte’s old school-era band, The Farewell 42s.

    Obviously, the AI technology is still quite early (the vocals are very auto-tuney) but it’s quite mind-blowing that I can generate a competent (almost passable) song within literally minutes. The app also allows you to remix, add more to the song, etc. It’s addicting. I’m going through a phase, much like I did last year when I discovered an app for generating AI artwork based on your own prompts. This app also auto-generates a thumbnail image too, based on the lyrics.

    The sequel

    A brief 2020 summary: there was a global pandemic; I was off work for months during lockdowns; Donald Trump lost the US election; nothing much happened but also everything happened. For Christmas, I got lots of chocolate, sweets, money, vouchers, new pyjamas, jumpers and tops. It was a comfy, low-key day.

    I watched the New Year’s London light show. They incorporated drones and lights across various landmarks, and it made a change from the usual firework explosion centred around the London Eye. Had a chill day watching YouTube videos, and nibbles for tea. We’re in Tier 4 now – basically Lockdown III.
