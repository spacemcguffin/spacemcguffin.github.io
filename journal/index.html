11/02/22 — ephemerality

2:30am. The night sky is so clear tonight. Snow is dusting the tops of houses around me. So pretty. Absolute silence, and peace. Momentary bliss. Being a night owl isn’t always a bad thing.

We all get so wrapped up in politics and work and technology and anxiety and other people that we forget just how beautiful this planet and this universe is.

There’s something quite fairytale about snow-dusted rooftops against a starry sky. It’s nice to just let go and feel like a kid again. A world full of wonder and magic. I should enjoy the night sky more often. It does wonders for the soul.

22/08/21

There are so many years of my life now that I don’t have any record of.

I remember milestones and certain moments but the past is becoming hazier and hazier in my mind, and I feel like I’ve lost a grasp on so much of who I used to be; my childhood, and anything of note beyond the last decade (which, itself, has largely been a blur).

I’ve got a handful of photos to look back on, but all my old tweets are gone, my old diary is lost to time, and my memory is slowly fading to white. And in some respects, that’s okay, that’s good, you have to keep moving forward, but I also feel some sadness that I struggle to remember how I got here, what I used to feel, what I used to like, who I used to be.
