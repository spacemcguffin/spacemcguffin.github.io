equilibrium

the solitary car on the country lane
out of the dark, for a second, returning
as the town clock strikes in the dead of night.
a glimpse of life, a moment of remembrance
the tiniest memory of normality, resemblance.

i feel the rain for the first time in a fortnight
the veil of the world and the clouds dispersing
uncovering the ringing of the midnight hour

she’s closed for business for the time being
but she will reopen, stronger, and healthier
her children much steadier, course correction,
this is the time for human reflection.
